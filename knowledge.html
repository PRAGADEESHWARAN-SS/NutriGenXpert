Knowledge

What It Does

  • Genetic Predispositions: Key insights into how your body processes nutrients, your metabolic efficiency, and health risks encoded in your DNA.
  • Gut Microbiome Composition: The balance of bacteria and microorganisms in your gut that influence digestion, immunity, and overall health.
  • Biochemical Markers: Real-time data on your vitamin levels, hormones, and metabolic function, providing a snapshot of your current health status.

Why It Matters

  • Genetic variations influence how you metabolize nutrients and your risk for certain health conditions, enabling a diet plan aligned with your DNA.
  • Gut health plays a crucial role in digestion, nutrient absorption, and immunity. By understanding your microbiome, we can recommend foods that support a healthy gut.
  • Biomarkers offer insights into how your body is functioning in real-time, allowing for precise, data-driven adjustments to your nutrition plan.

Together, these insights ensure that your diet is fully personalized, addressing not just your genetic makeup but also how your body functions and adapts.

How It Works

  • Genetic Test: A simple cheek swab provides data to analyze your DNA for personalized dietary guidance.
  • Microbiome Test: A stool sample helps map the microorganisms in your gut to understand your digestive and immune health.
  • Biochemical Test: Blood or urine samples measure key biomarkers such as vitamins, hormones, and metabolism to fine-tune your nutrition plan.
